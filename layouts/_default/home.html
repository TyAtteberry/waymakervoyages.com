Common Questions

Everything you need to know before you set sail

01

What age range is this for?

We generally recommend voyages for children ages 8 and up, as this is when they're typically ready to engage fully in the family activities, treasure hunts, snorkeling, and sailing experiences. That said, every family is unique — we've had younger children join us, and we're happy to work with you to design a purposeful, age-appropriate adventure that fits your family's needs. For father–son or mother–daughter journeys, we've found the ideal age range to be 11–18. These trips are especially meaningful when a church group, circle of friends, or group of parents brings children of similar ages to share the experience together. No matter the ages, our goal is to create an intentional, safe, and unforgettable voyage where every child feels valued — and every parent feels proud of the memories they're building.

02

Do I need sailing experience?

No sailing experience is required. Our professional skippers take care of all the navigation and boat handling, so you can simply relax and enjoy the journey. For anyone who wants to learn, they love teaching — from the basics of steering and trimming sails to understanding how the wind moves the boat. You can participate as much or as little as you'd like.

03

Is this a Christian trip?

We follow Jesus Christ, and our voyages are shaped by that foundation. At the same time, we thoughtfully tailor each trip to the comfort level and preferences of our guests. Our approach is subtle, meaningful, and rooted in experiencing God's creation in a natural, authentic way. It's common for a voyage to include a short daily scripture that relates to the day's adventure, a prayer shared around the dinner table, worship music playing softly in the background, or guided conversations that open the door to deeper understanding. Nothing is forced, but the opportunity for spiritual growth is always present. Most guests tell us they appreciate how faith is woven into the journey — gently, intentionally, and in a way that brings families closer to God and to one another.

04

What if I get seasick?

The BVI are known for their calm, protected waters and short sailing hops between islands, which greatly reduces the chance of seasickness. Still, we recommend bringing whatever motion-sickness remedies work best for you, and we'll walk you through simple tips and best practices before we ever leave the dock. Your safety and comfort are our top priorities, and we always have backup routes and options if conditions change.

05

What's the payment and refund policy?

A deposit is required at the time of booking to reserve your yacht and hold your week. The remaining balance is due several months before departure. Refunds follow standard charter terms: as your trip date approaches, payments become less refundable because yachts are reserved exclusively for your group. If plans change, we'll do everything we can to help you reschedule. Before any payment, we walk you through the full timeline, cost structure, and refund windows so you have complete clarity and confidence.

06

Can I gift a voyage to my pastor or another family?

Absolutely — and it's one of the most meaningful gifts you can give. We've helped congregations honor their pastors, families create multi-generational memories, and friends surprise couples who needed time to reconnect. We'll work with you on every detail, including a beautiful presentation package. Just reach out through our contact form or call us directly.
